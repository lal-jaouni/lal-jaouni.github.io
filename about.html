About Me

Scientist turned engineer.

I started my career in a tissue engineering lab at Virginia Tech, studying how astrocytes remodel brain tissue after traumatic injury. I spent my days synthesizing hydrogels, running atomic force microscopy, and staining cells for fluorescence imaging. That experience taught me how to think about complex systems, design rigorous experiments, and work across disciplines.

After finishing my M.S. (4.0 GPA, Summa Cum Laude), I joined Meso Scale Diagnostics, where the work shifted from bench science to building the software and automation systems that power diagnostics R&D. I've since written 166,000+ lines of production code across 12+ projects — from full-stack lab information management systems to embedded motion controllers to AI platforms integrating 100+ LLM providers.

What drives me is building tools for domains where the problems are real and the stakes are high. Clinical data, laboratory instruments, scientific workflows — these aren't toy problems, and they benefit from engineers who understand the science underneath.
