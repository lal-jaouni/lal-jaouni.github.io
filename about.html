About Me

Healthcare AI engineer, building systems clinicians trust.

I started my career in a tissue engineering lab at Virginia Tech, studying how astrocytes remodel brain tissue after traumatic injury. That work taught me to respect complexity, design rigorous experiments, and work across disciplines. More importantly, it showed me that the most important problems often lie at the intersection of biology, engineering, and systems thinking.

After my M.S. (4.0 GPA, Summa Cum Laude), I joined a diagnostics R&D company where I shifted from bench science to building production ML systems, laboratory information management platforms, and embedded control systems. I've since written 166,000+ lines of code across 12+ projects. More recently, I started working on the DARPA triage challenge, where I'm building an end-to-end ML system (0.938 AUROC) predicting life-saving clinical interventions from multi-modal data across Level-1 trauma centers. That work crystallized my focus: healthcare AI at scale.

Today, my work spans three areas: building production ML systems for clinical deployment (physiological signal processing, FDA regulatory pathways, model validation); understanding healthcare problems at scale (I built a market intelligence pipeline to systematically evaluate 25+ healthcare AI opportunities from 6,700+ signals); and exploring what's next in this space. I'm open to technical advisory roles, healthcare AI consulting engagements, and conversations with founding teams solving real healthcare problems.
