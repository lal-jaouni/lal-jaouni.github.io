DARPA Triage Challenge

An end-to-end machine learning system that predicts life-saving clinical interventions from multi-modal trauma data. Built as a consultant through MSAI Labs, processing 327 GB of raw clinical data across 7,287 patients from two Level-1 trauma centers, achieving 0.938 AUROC across 25 intervention targets.

The Problem

In trauma care, the first minutes matter most. Paramedics and first responders have to make rapid decisions about which interventions a patient needs — intubation, blood transfusion, chest tube, surgery — often with incomplete information. The DARPA Triage Challenge asks: can machine learning predict which interventions a patient will need, using data available in the field?

The data is messy, multi-modal, and massive. Electronic health records, physiological waveforms (ECG, PPG, arterial blood pressure, capnography), lab results, vitals, and clinical notes — all collected across two Level-1 trauma centers with different recording systems and conventions.

What I Built

Data Pipeline & Feature Engineering

Processed 327 GB of raw clinical data into a structured ML-ready format. This involved parsing 179,415 time segments across 7,287 patients, building a signal quality assessment framework (7 metrics per signal type, 353,192 quality records), and engineering 433+ features across three tiers: minimal (30), basic (110), and full (433).

A key early finding: 56% of arterial blood pressure data was physiologically implausible. Discovering and handling that required building custom quality scoring for each signal type — SNR, kurtosis, zero-crossing rate, template matching, spectral quality, and physiological plausibility checks.

Tabular Classification

The core prediction system uses per-target XGBoost models with Bayesian hyperparameter optimization (Optuna, 50 trials per target). Across 25 ICD-compliant intervention targets, the system achieves a mean AUROC of 0.938, with 22 of 25 targets above 0.90.

Waveform Deep Learning

For physiological waveform analysis, I built a PatchTST transformer (2.1M parameters) and a U-Net baseline for arterial blood pressure prediction. The two-stage calibration pipeline (frozen encoder + XGBoost head) achieves 0.952 waveform correlation with AAMI-compliant blood pressure error (SBP 4.9 mmHg, DBP 2.7 mmHg). Pre-training on 5.09M PulseDB segments with a U2-Net (44M parameters) established the foundation for transfer learning.

Signal Processing

Developed processing pipelines for four signal types: PPG, ECG, arterial blood pressure, and capnography. Each pipeline handles ADC-to-physical unit calibration, peak detection, beat segmentation, and feature extraction including 168 wavelet features via discrete wavelet transform and 44 clinical features from waveform morphology.

Experiment Rigor

Ran 16 formal experiments with ablation analysis and produced 13 executive reports. Every modeling decision was tracked through MLflow, with SHAP-based interpretability for clinical validation. The codebase includes 437+ test cases across 26 files (4,726 lines of test code) and 100+ pages of documentation.

Why This Matters

The DARPA triage challenge represents real clinical impact: faster, more accurate decisions in the first hours after trauma can mean the difference between recovery and permanent disability. But more importantly, it demonstrates what production healthcare AI requires: dealing with messy, multi-modal data from multiple sites; building quality assurance for clinical signals; navigating FDA-relevant considerations around validation and reproducibility; and shipping code that clinicians actually trust enough to use in high-stakes settings.

Working with real clinical data is fundamentally different from clean ML benchmarks. More than half my time went into understanding the data — discovering that 56% of ABP waveforms were physiologically implausible, building quality metrics that could quantify "trustworthy" signals, and engineering features that capture clinical meaning rather than just statistical patterns. The project reinforced that in healthcare ML, the data pipeline is the model.
