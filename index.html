About

Scientist turned engineer, focused on healthcare AI.

I started in a tissue engineering lab at Virginia Tech, studying how brain cells respond to traumatic injury. That research on complex biological systems led me into healthcare AI, where I now build production ML systems that handle real clinical data, FDA regulations, and the specifics of physiological signals.

What I care about is building tools for domains where the problems are real and the stakes are high. I'm currently working on the DARPA triage challenge—processing physiological waveforms and clinical records from trauma centers. I also built a market intelligence pipeline to systematically understand which healthcare AI problems matter most to clinicians. I'm open to advisory, consulting, and cofounder conversations where technical depth, healthcare domain knowledge, and regulatory thinking matter.
