About

Scientist turned engineer. Each phase built on the last.

I started in a tissue engineering lab at Virginia Tech, generating data with atomic force microscopes and rheometers. That led me to build the instruments and automation systems that collect data, then the data pipelines and LIMS that organize it, and now the ML models that learn from it.

I understand the data because I've generated it at the bench. I understand the instruments because I've programmed their firmware. I understand the pipelines because I've built them at scale. And I understand the models because I know what the data actually means. Right now I'm applying all of it on a DARPA-funded challenge, predicting life-saving interventions from streaming physiological data.
