About

Scientist turned engineer, focused on healthcare AI.

I started in a tissue engineering lab at Virginia Tech, studying how brain cells respond to traumatic injury. That research on complex biological systems led me into healthcare AI, where I now build production ML systems that handle real clinical data, FDA regulations, and the specifics of physiological signals.

My focus: building AI systems for healthcare that regulators trust and clinicians actually use. I'm currently working on biosignal processing (ECG, PPG, ABP) systems through the DARPA triage challenge (0.938 AUROC), and I built a market intelligence pipeline to systematically evaluate healthcare AI opportunities at scale. I'm exploring technical advisory roles, healthcare AI consulting, and potential cofounder opportunities where my combination of technical depth, healthcare domain knowledge, and regulatory expertise adds the most value.
